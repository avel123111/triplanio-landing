This Notice covers our role as the controller of the personal information that you and the people you invite to your trips enter into the Service. Where we use third-party providers (for example, our hosting platform or payment processor), they act as our processors except where otherwise stated.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account information

When you create an account, we collect basic identity information from the sign-in method you use, such as your email address, your name, and, where provided, a profile picture URL. We also store your in-app preferences (UI language, theme, email-notification settings, current subscription status and, for Pro subscribers, the subscription end date).

1.2 Trip content

We store the content you create in the Service, including trip titles, descriptions, dates, notes, city visits, hotels, transfers, activities (including times, locations, and addresses), documents you attach, budget categories and expenses (including amounts, currencies, and how a cost is split among members), hotel proposals, comments, and votes. This content is associated with the trip and is visible to the trip members in accordance with their role.

1.3 Collaboration and invitations

When you invite someone to a trip, we store the invitee’s email address, the display name you give them, who invited them, when they accepted, and their role and status. We also store the same information for “offline” placeholder members you add without an email. You should not add personal information about other people that they would not want stored in the Service.

1.4 Communications and notifications

We send transactional emails (for example, trip invitations, role changes, hotel-proposal updates, trip reminders), and we store records of those communications so we can support you and avoid duplicates. You can opt out of non-essential email notifications in your account settings; essential service emails (for example, security and account-status messages) cannot be turned off while you have an account.

1.5 Payment information

If you purchase a Pro plan, your payment is handled by Stripe, Inc. (“Stripe”). Stripe collects your payment-card details directly under its own privacy policy. We do not receive or store your full card number. From Stripe we receive a customer identifier, your billing email, the plan you purchased, the status of your subscription, and a record of relevant events (checkout completed, subscription created, updated, cancelled, payment failed, refunded). We use this information to grant or revoke your Pro entitlement and to support you.

1.6 Telegram integration (optional)

If you connect a Telegram chat to a trip, we store the Telegram chat ID associated with you and that trip, the integration’s “active” status, and the contents of trip notifications we send to that chat. The Telegram service itself is operated by a third party and processes messages under its own terms.

1.7 AI-planner inputs and outputs

When you use the AI trip planner, the text of your prompt, your prior conversation with the planner in the same session, the current draft trip, and your chosen response language are sent to a third-party large-language-model provider through our hosting platform. We store the resulting draft if you save it to a trip. Do not include in your AI prompts any sensitive personal data, yours or anyone else’s, that you do not want sent to the AI provider.

1.8 Location, mapping, and reference data

Place names and addresses you enter are sent to Google Maps Platform (including Places autocomplete and geocoding) so we can show suggestions and map links. We also fetch city cover images and short descriptions from Wikipedia/Wikimedia, and currency exchange rates from the Frankfurter API for the trip budget. These third parties may receive your IP address and request metadata when their services are loaded by your browser.

1.9 Technical and usage data

When you access the Service, our hosting platform and infrastructure providers automatically receive technical information such as your IP address, browser type, device and operating-system information, language, time-zone, the pages and features you accessed, and the timestamps of those accesses. We use this information to operate, secure, and debug the Service.

1.10 Cookies and local storage

We use cookies and similar browser storage (including localStorage) that are strictly necessary to keep you signed in, remember your interface preferences (such as language and theme), keep certain in-app state between visits, and protect the Service from abuse. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies. Some third-party services we rely on (for example, Stripe and Google Maps) may set their own cookies when their components load.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information described above to:

  • Provide and maintain the Service, including authenticating you, displaying your trips, sharing trips with members you invite, and remembering your preferences.
  • Process payments and manage Pro subscriptions through Stripe.
  • Send transactional emails and, if you opt in, optional notifications such as trip reminders or Telegram messages.
  • Generate AI trip suggestions in response to your prompts.
  • Operate, monitor, secure, and improve the Service, including diagnosing problems, preventing abuse, and detecting fraud.
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations and respond to lawful requests from public authorities.
  • Enforce our Terms of Use and protect the rights, safety, and property of us, our users, and third parties.

3. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We share information only in the following circumstances:

  • With other trip members. Information you enter in a trip is visible to the members of that trip in accordance with their role (owner, admin, viewer), including their names, emails, expenses, votes, and comments.
  • Through public share links. If you generate a public read-only link for a trip, anyone who knows the link can view the read-only trip content (without member emails) until you revoke the link.
  • With our service providers, who process information on our behalf under contractual confidentiality and security commitments. These include our hosting platform (Base44), our payment processor (Stripe), the third-party LLM provider used by the AI planner (accessed through Base44), the email-delivery provider, Google Maps Platform, Wikipedia/Wikimedia, the Frankfurter exchange-rate service, and — if you choose to enable it — Telegram.
  • When required by law, including to comply with a subpoena or court order, to respond to a lawful government request, or to investigate suspected fraud, security, or abuse incidents.
  • In connection with a corporate transaction such as a merger, acquisition, financing, or asset sale, where personal information may be transferred as part of the assets. We will use reasonable efforts to ensure that the recipient honours the commitments in this Notice.

4. International Data Transfers

The third-party providers we rely on operate globally and may process your information in countries other than the one in which you live, including countries that may have different data-protection laws. Where applicable, we rely on the safeguards offered by those providers (for example, standard contractual clauses or similar mechanisms) to protect international transfers.

5. Data Retention

We keep your personal information for as long as your account is active and for as long as we need it to provide the Service and to comply with our legal obligations. When you delete your account, we permanently delete the trips you own and the related child records (hotels, activities, transfers, city visits, budget categories and expenses, budget segment shares, documents, services, member rows, notifications addressed to you, and your subscription records) shortly after the deletion request, and we remove you as a member of any trips owned by other users.

Some information may be retained for a limited additional period in backups, security and abuse-prevention logs, and in records that we are required to keep for legal, accounting, or tax reasons (for example, Stripe transaction records). One-time Pro Trip purchases are recorded as informational entries even after account deletion, as they are not subject to ongoing billing.

An active recurring Stripe subscription (Pro Monthly or Pro Yearly) must be cancelled in the Stripe billing portal before account deletion; this prevents future billing for a deleted account.

6. Your Rights

Depending on the laws applicable to you, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access — to ask whether we hold personal information about you and to receive a copy.
  • Rectification — to ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Erasure — to ask us to delete your information, including by using the in-app account-deletion flow.
  • Restriction or objection — to ask us to restrict or stop certain uses of your information.
  • Portability — to receive your information in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, where this right applies.
  • Withdraw consent — where we rely on your consent, to withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of earlier processing.
  • Complaint — to lodge a complaint with the data-protection authority in your country, where applicable.

To exercise any of these rights, you can use the in-app settings (for example, the “Delete account” flow and the notification preferences) or contact us at info@triplanio.com. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

7. Security

We rely on our hosting platform and on commonly used industry safeguards — including TLS-encrypted transport, scoped database access, hashed/managed credentials at the identity provider, and server-side authorisation checks — to protect personal information. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, however, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you have reason to believe that your account or our Service has been compromised, please contact us at info@triplanio.com.

8. Children

The Service is not intended for children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If you believe that a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at info@triplanio.com and we will take steps to delete that information.

9. Third-Party Links and Content

Trips may contain links to third-party websites (for example, hotel booking pages, attractions, or maps). When you click such a link, you leave the Service and are subject to the destination website’s own terms and privacy policy. We are not responsible for the content or practices of third-party sites.

10. Changes to this Notice

We may update this Privacy Notice from time to time. The most current version will always be posted in the Service and the “Effective date” at the top will be updated. If a change is material, we will use reasonable efforts to give you notice through the Service or by email before it takes effect.

11. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Notice or about your personal information, please contact us at info@triplanio.com.
